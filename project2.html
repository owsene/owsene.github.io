Fun facts (you might not know) about cats. by Amy Kuras

Only one cat has been in outerspace.

Félicette was a stray Parisian tuxedo cat who is the only cat to have been successfully launched into space. France wanted to try cats in space because the other superpowers had used dogs or monkeys, hoping to discover something new. She was launched on October 18, 1963 in a French space program.

Cats prowl the House of Mouse.

There are around 200 cats that live in Disneyland and they live there for pest control of mice—that aren’t named Mickey. They are spayed and neutered but on the off chance kittens are born, they are adopted out to people who work at Disney. These cats are difficult to see during the day at Disney as they tend to avoid people. Keep your eyes peeled for them next time you’re at Disney!

In the ancient times, cats were worshipped as gods. (they won't let us forget it)

Cat brains are more like humans than dogs.

Believe it or not, cats brains share 90 percent similarity with the human brain. Their brain is more biologically similar to a humans than a dogs. Both humans and cats have identical regions in their brains that are responsible for their emotions. Cats are thought to have a learning style similar to that of a 2 year old toddler. They have complex brains, good short-term memory, high emotional intelligence, and the recognition of object permanence.

They have...special tastes.

Ever wonder why your cat likes to drink from sinks? Cats have a special taste receptor for water, and so they can taste differences better than humans can. Also, they don’t have a taste receptor for sweetness, so they tend to not be interested in sweet treats. Though they can taste scents! Which is why sometimes they will sit there with their mouth open just tasting the air.
